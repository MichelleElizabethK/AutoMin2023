SUMMARY
- The European Parliament delegation to the Conciliation Committee approved the joint text approved by the Committee for a regulation of the European Parliament and the Council on common rules in the field of civil aviation security and repealing Regulation No 2320/2002.
- On 4 March the Council formally completed the third reading of the new framework regulation on aviation security.
- It is now up to members of the European Parliament to adopt it.
- The European Parliament will have greater power over the general rules the Commission has to draw up after the adoption of the framework regulation.
- After long negotiations, Parliament and the Council have reached an agreement on common security standards for Europe's airports and airlines.
- However, they did not agree on the financing of the security measures.
- The rapporteur and Commissioner Barrot will present a separate proposal on this matter within the next few months The European Parliament and the European Commission have been debating aviation security for several years.
- The agreement reached in Singapore is not perfect, but represents an important step forward.
- It is a great pity that the same approach is not taken with regard to duty-free goods and transit passengers.
- The amendment to the Regulation establishing common rules in the field of civil aviation security has been reached.
- It provides an effective framework for the safe movement of passengers in a globalised environment, while enabling Member States to adapt their policy to new developments and the special needs that may arise during a particular PERSON129: Madam President, air transport needs defined legal rules, but they must be as flexible as possible and at the same time sufficiently harmonised, especially with regard to flight security.
- First, the charges for air security must be transparent and relate only to actual cost.
- Second The rapporteur, Mr Costa, has achieved an agreement on two fundamental topics: who pays for security and how to improve transparency in the cost-sharing between taxpayers and users and on transfers of rights.
- The Commission has adopted a regulation aimed at striking a reasonable balance between the need to protect passengers against terrorist activity and the operational aspects at airports in order to avoid over-complicated checking procedures.
- The regulation allows for derogations for non-member countries and for the detection of explosives in liquids.
- The debate is closed.
- The vote will take place on Tuesday, 11 March 2008.
