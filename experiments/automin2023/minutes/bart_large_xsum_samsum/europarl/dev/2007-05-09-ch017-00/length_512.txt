SUMMARY
- The Committee on Internal Market and Consumer Protection recommends that President Verheugen adopt the Council common position, modified by some amendments.
- Parliament will vote tomorrow for the three amendments adopted by the Commission on Internal Market and Consumer Protection.
- The Vice-President of the Commission, De, thanks Mr Toubon for his constructive work on the legislative proposal and agrees with everything he said.
- De hopes that the Council will follow Parliament's example and carry out an impact assessment when making amendments to legislative proposals.
- Regulations from the 70s and 80s were laid down for prepacked products.
- They were adopted to open up the internal market for goods and to ensure consumer protection.
- Now it is time to simplify and harmonise the packaging sizes.
- The Commission will review the situation in a few years' The Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection will support the amendments tomorrow.
- Jacques Toubon has led an outstanding review of the process.
- The Committee wants to remove unnecessary legislation, much of which dates back 20 or 30 years.
- They want to give manufacturers the flexibility to produce products in different sizes The European Parliament is debating a new law regarding prices in units of litres or kilograms in supermarkets.
- According to the new law, nominal quantities of bread packaging will not be abolished automatically after a certain time, but the European Commission will assess the consequences and possibilities beforehand and respond to any disruption of the market.
- The ALDE Group of Liberals and Democrats for Europe voted in favour of transitional periods for certain categories of product.
- They did not want a revision clause in the actual body of the Directive, but they will support the amendments proposed by rapporteur Toubon.
- The debate on the Packaging Directive is closed.
- The vote will take place tomorrow.
