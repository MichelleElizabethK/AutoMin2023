SUMMARY
- The Committee on Internal Market and Consumer Protection on the Council common position for adopting a Directive 76/211/EEC (13484/1/2006 - C6-0039/2007 - (Rapporteur: Jacques Toubon).
- The Vice-President of the Commission, De, thanks rapporteur Toubon for his constructive work on the legislative proposal.
- De hopes that the Council will follow Parliament's example and carry out an impact assessment when making amendments to legislative proposals.
- De is convinced that the proposal will have a positive impact The Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection will support the amendments tomorrow.
- Jacques Toubon has led an outstanding review of the process.
- The Committee wants to remove unnecessary legislation, much of which dates back 20 or 30 years.
- They want to give manufacturers the flexibility to produce products in different sizes The European Parliament is debating a new law concerning packaging sizes for bread in the UK.
- The ALDE Group, the Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe, supports the idea of transitional periods for certain categories of product.
- The Swedish Members of the Christian Democrats and European Democrats are opposed to a Packaging Directive.
- The vote will take place tomorrow.
