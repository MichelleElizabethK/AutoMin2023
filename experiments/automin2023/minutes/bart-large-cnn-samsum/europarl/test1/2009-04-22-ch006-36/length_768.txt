SUMMARY
- The European Parliament has called for the signing of a new trade agreement with Turkmenistan to be linked to respect for human rights.
- The European Commission is aware of Parliament's concerns regarding the possibility of suspending the agreements.
- The Commission is committed to taking appropriate measures in conformity with the agreement, including, if necessary, the suspension of the agreement.
- Parliament has been debating Turkmenistan for three-and-a-half years in various committees in this House.
- Parliament's demands have not been met by the Commission and the Council.
- The President-in-Office of the Council expressed no opinion as to how the Council would react if Parliament were to call for the suspension of the agreement.
- Parliament obtained the assurances that are possible within the framework of the European Treaties.
