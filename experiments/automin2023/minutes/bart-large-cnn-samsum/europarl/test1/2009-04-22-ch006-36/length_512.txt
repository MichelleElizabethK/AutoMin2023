SUMMARY
- The Council has always examined the opinions and resolutions of Parliament with great interest and attention.
- The Council has taken the issue of human rights very seriously, as evidenced in the contractual relations of the European Union with countries in various parts of the world.
- There have been more than 15 cases since 1995 when the Community has partially or fully suspended the application of agreements in relation to some of the countries for non-execution of obligations relating to the human rights clause.
- In Turkmenistan, the Council will continue to push for the release of political prisoners, for free access of the International Committee of the Red Cross to prisons and for lifting of the restrictions on foreign travel.
- The European Parliament has concerns about the democracy and human rights situation in Turkmenistan.
- The Interim Trade Agreement (ITA) and PCA (Article 2) contain human rights clauses which constitute an essential element of those agreements.
- The Commission is aware of Parliament's concerns regarding the possibility of suspending the agreements.
- It will consider Parliament's recommendations in its proposal to the Council.
- Parliament has been debating Turkmenistan for three-and-a-half years in various committees in this House.
- Parliament's demands have not been met by the Commission and the Council.
- The President-in-Office of the Council expressed no opinion as to how the Council would react if Parliament were to call for the suspension of the agreement.
- Parliament obtained the assurances that are possible within the framework of the European Treaties.
