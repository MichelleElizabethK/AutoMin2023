SUMMARY
- The next item is the report by Jutta Haug on the 2009 Preliminary Draft Budget and mandate for the conciliation - Section III - Commission (2008/2025), on the Committee on Budgets' report on the preliminary draft budget for 2009.
- The European Parliament is preparing itself for the amended procedure after the Reform Treaty.
- The resolution 'First reflections on the 2009 Preliminary Draft Budget and mandate for the conciliation' was adopted unopposed by the Committee on Budgets.
- It criticises the preliminary draft budget with which the Commission has presented them and finds much in it to criticise.
- Parliament wants to implement the European priorities.
- The 2009 Preliminary Draft Budget was prepared by the Commission within the limits of the financial programming period 2007-2013.
- The Commission will table an amendment to the draft, providing accurate calculations for the financing of Palestine and Kosovo in the near future.
- For the first time, there is a list of new pilot projects and preparatory actions at this early stage.
- Next week there will be a tripartite meeting and the first conciliation meeting to prepare for first reading in the Council.
- PERSON078 is dissatisfied with heading 4 of the draft budget.
- Heading 4 is only 1.8% up on the 2008 budget, which is below the average budget increase of 3.1%.
- The portion reserved for external relations and development was cut in the preliminary draft budget this year.
- Head 4 does not have enough funds for the Mediterranean and food aid.
- PERSON079 is rapporteur for the budget for development cooperation.
- He is concerned about the food crisis and results-oriented evaluation of development aid.
- He thinks that too little attention is being paid to food security and food production in developing countries.
- He supports the proposal by the European Commission to use underspends from agriculture for this purpose.
- There is a discrepancy between the Commission's Annual Policy Strategy and what is being proposed in the budget on climate and energy policy.
- The Committee on Industry, Research and Energy is concerned at the inadequate margin, 82 million under heading 1A.
- The Commission proposes 30 million as a first allocation for the energy and climate policy, but it is proposed that money be taken from the cooperation programme of the Seventh Framework Programme and its energy section.
- PERSON082 thanks Mrs Haug for a good report on the Preliminary Draft Budget for the agriculture budget.
- The Committee on Agriculture and Rural Development is concerned about rural development measures.
- The People's Party wants value to be generated from budgetary expenditure and wants to act in the interests of greater efficiency.
- This week Parliament is deciding on the political priorities of the budget for 2009.
- The rapporteur, Mrs Haug, has managed to anticipate the stages of the budgetary procedure.
- She has helped Parliament to understand and discuss the priorities of parliamentary committees and political groups.
- She deplores the Council's lack of ambition and systematic nature of the cuts to the Commission's preliminary draft budget.
- The 2009 EU budget must reflect political priorities.
- EU Heads of State or Government have adopted an ambitious energy-policy agenda.
- Frontex is chronically underfunded.
- The Socialist Group in the European Parliament proposes transferring underspends from the agriculture budget to the areas in which the budget is too tight.
- The EU budget is 1% of Europe's GDP, compared to 20% of GDP committed by the U.S. at federal level.
- The Commission is taking 10% of this percentage for climate change.
- UNFCCC recommends mobilising between 0.6 and 1.6% of the GDP to combat climate change and the World Bank mentions 2%.
- To help developing countries, USD 100 billion a year would be needed to finance all these projects.
- The EU has a margin of at least EUR 2 billion.
- The Commission's budget is very austere.
- The least flexibility is in section four.
- The sums to be given to Palestine and Kosovo in 2009 will not fit into the budgetary framework.
- The amount for security and defence policy that serve the militarisation of the EU do not belong to the common budget.
- Member States' governments are not drawing on budgetary resources.
- The Haug report presents the first reflections on the Preliminary Draft Budget.
- The plenary mandate for the conciliation will take place on 17 July.
- Without the Reform Treaty, some key points of future-oriented common policy are not yet in place.
- The European Council has presented an intensive programme for revising the multiannual financial programming.
- The EU budget is not equal to the EU's needs and ambitions.
- It does not reflect the political priorities of the EU.
- The French Presidency's representative has already disappeared from the budget.
- The percentage of payment appropriations is 0.9%, which is one of the lowest in recent years.
- The amount of funds for commitments in the 2009 budget is 1.04% of GNI, and just 0.9% for payments, which is a fall of 3.3% in comparison with payments in 2008.
- The funds mentioned under heading 4 are just 1.8% higher than in 2008, even though it has been apparent for years that this area is permanently under-financed.
- The prices of basic agricultural raw materials have risen by several dozen percent recently.
- PERSON094 is in the process of setting a clear mandate for negotiations on the discussions with the Council next week.
- The four main issues that concern citizens are high prices, climate change, competitiveness and environment, Frontex and immigration.
- The draft budget for 2009 allows for the consolidation of programmes approved for the financial period of 2007 to 2013 and puts new emphasis on the need to resolve urgent issues.
- PERSON096 has enormous respect for Mrs Haug's presentation of the budgetary report.
- He wants to avoid sacrificing the appropriations of sub-heading 3b.
- He also wants the Council and its Presidency to avoid making cuts in the Preliminary Draft Budget at first reading.
- The European Commission has prepared a draft budget for 2009.
- The amount of commitment appropriations is increasing by 3.1%, but the amount of payment appropriations for cohesion policy is decreasing by 14%.
- The IMCO Committee requests additional funding for the SOLVIT network and supports the further funding of the projects regarding consumers, market research, and market supervision measures.
- According to PERSON099, the report of the European Parliament regarding the preliminary draft budget for 2009 should indicate the political priorities of the EU at present.
- The report has largely emphasized the budgetary insufficiencies regarding the fight against global warming, but not enough on the food crisis that European Union citizens are dealing with.
- The only instrument by which we can respond efficiently to the situation is the common agricultural policy.

- They want to set something in motion to avoid a static budget and move the Council.
- The vote will take place on 8 July 2008.
