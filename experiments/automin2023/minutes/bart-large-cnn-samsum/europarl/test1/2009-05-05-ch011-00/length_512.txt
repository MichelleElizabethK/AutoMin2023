- The next item is Question Time (B6-0231/2009).
- The Commission considers non-transparent systems of income taxation within the European Union to be compatible with free competition.
- If not, it intends to put a stop to such systems in Member States or parts of Member States.
- Member States have agreed on several measures proposed by the European Commission to tackle the erosion of tax bases and investment allocation distortions.
- EU-wide cooperation and fair tax competition rules are vital to protect revenues.
- The European Commission adopted a communication on 28 April 2009 to promote good governance in the tax area.
- PERSON297 missed a reference to recent agreements in the framework of the G20 concerning the fight against tax havens and so-called 'non-cooperative jurisdictions'.
- The Commission is a full participant in the G 20 and is participating fully in the process.
- In principle, PERSON299 is against the introduction of new taxes.
- However, they must consider taxes in conjunction with short-term stock profits.
- The Lisbon Treaty is clear on how the competences are divided in text.
- The Commission has no intention of writing a new treaty with different responsibilities.
- In November 2007 Parliament adopted resolution (P6_TA(2007)0507) on an integrated approach to earthquakes by the EU.
- In the wake of the recent deadly earthquake in Italy, does the Commission intend to take further initiatives to protect European citizens from earthquakes?
- The Commission is committed to helping to protect people from earthquakes.
- The Commission recently adopted a communication entitled 'A Community approach on the prevention of natural and manmade disasters' which also covers earthquakes.
- PERSON304 questions why the Commission refuses to do for earthquakes what it did for floods.

- The Commission proposes measures to tackle the serious energy and economic challenges facing the European Union.
- Solar thermal electricity is not included in the list of sectors which stand to benefit.
- European industry is currently the world leader in this area, but it can only maintain this position if the domestic market expands and if further R&D is promoted.
- There is an opportunity to provide 300 million emission allowances for new entrants from next year.
- Renewable energy projects, such as solar thermal electricity, are eligible.
- The Commission will table a new communication on financing low-carbon technologies in the summer or early autumn.
- In the event that the projects nominated under the Recovery Plan are not in a position to draw down the funding by 30 June 2010, there will be a reserve list of projects that are ready to go.
- There are plans to build nuclear power stations in many EU countries.
- The Commission will consider making a necessary proposal if part of the money is available.
- Each project is analysed on its own merits based on the Community acquis and on environmental legislation.
- There are also requirements of the environmental impact assessment.
- PERSON315 wants to know if an ex-ante study can be carried out on the basis of EuroMediterranean cooperation and the particular character of the area.

- In his own home city of Cork, local authorities and businesses are striving towards the creation of sustainable energy policies.
- The European Commission supports the Covenant of Mayors, which requires cities to reduce CO2 by more than 20% by 2020.
- The Commission negotiates with other national, regional and local administrations to ensure their support for the smaller cities.
- The Benchmarks of Excellence is intended to be implemented this year.
- EU cohesion policy provides significant support to cities to implement sustainable energy policies and actions.
- The Southern and Eastern Operational Programme, which includes the City of Cork, will invest EUR 669 million in the region and contribute to reaching Lisbon growth and jobs objectives.
- The Regional Development Fund was recently changed to give Member States the possibility to use regional funds for energy efficiency improvements and the integration of renewable energy into existing housing.
- The Covenant of Mayors is a framework for cities that share an ambition to develop sustainable models for their citizens.
- By the end of this year PERSON322 is planning to develop a new energy efficiency action plan and a communication on the financing of low-carbon energy sources.
- The Covenant of Mayors will provide much-needed intellectual input and experience to the policies we need to formulate to achieve sustainable cities.
- The Commission is working to prevent the growth of protectionist tendencies in international trade negotiations.
- The European Union has taken a firm stance against straight protectionism since the outbreak of the economic crisis.
- Since the beginning of the current crisis, the EU has been committed to multilateralism, transparency and to open markets based on rules that benefit developed and developing countries alike.
- The EU has consistently argued that an ambitious and balanced Doha Round outcome is among the most important instruments in preventing trade protectionism and boosting the economy.
- Sustainable development and, in particular, environmental and social considerations are important components in the EU trade policy.
- There is no imminent risk of a generalised protectionist escalation.
- Russia, Indonesia and Argentina need close monitoring.
- Canada has one of the world's highest levels of Internet piracy.
- Canada's Copyright Act is in urgent need of revision to bring it into line with international standards.
- The Commission is concerned about Canada's proposed reform of its Copyright Act.
- The Commission will take up the matter with the Canadian authorities at the highest level and call for the revision of Canadian copyright law as soon as possible.
- A trade agreement will provide the legal framework with which to address some of the EU's trade irritants and issues with Canada.
