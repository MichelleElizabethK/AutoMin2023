SUMMARY
- The next item is the joint debate on the proposal for a Council decision on the conclusion of the Agreement between the European Community and the Republic of Mauritius on the short-stay visa waiver.
- The report by Mr Busuttil on behalf of the Committee on Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs is also discussed.
- There is an agreement between the European Union and its citizens and the citizens of six different countries.
- It provides for a short stay visa waiver between the EU and citizens of Mauritius, The Republic of Seychelles, Barbados, The Federation of St Kitts and Nevis, the Bahamas, and Antigua and Barbuda.
- It is valid for a maximum stay of 3 months within a 6-month period.
- The six non-EU countries were transferred from the negative list to the positive list of Regulation (EC) No 539/2001.
- They are Antigua and Barbuda, the Bahamas, Barbados, Mauritius, Saint Kitts and Nevis and the Seychelles.
- The visa waiver covers all categories of persons travelling for all kinds of purposes except for the purpose of carrying out a paid activity.
- The Confederal Group of the European United Left - Nordic Green Left is in favour of the EU agreements with these six countries.
- They are surprised that the agreements do not apply to the outermost regions of France.
