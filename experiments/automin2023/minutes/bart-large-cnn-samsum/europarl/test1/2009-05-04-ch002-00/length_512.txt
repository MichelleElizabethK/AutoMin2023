SUMMARY
- It is the fifth anniversary of the biggest enlargement in the history of the European Union.
- On 1 May 2004, seventy-five million people from ten countries in Central and Eastern Europe and the Mediterranean became citizens of the EU.
- In 2007, Romania and Bulgaria joined the EU as new members.
- The EU has gained in strength, diversity and cultural richness.
- The Treaty of Lisbon contains the reforms necessary to adapt the EU's institutions to the consequences of enlargement.
- Roxana Saberi has been sentenced to eight years in prison for allegedly spying on behalf of the US and is being held in the Evin prison in Tehran.
- European Parliament demands her immediate and unconditional release.
- The sentencing of Mrs Saberi comes at a critical moment, two months before the presidential elections in Iran and a short time after efforts were initiated by the US and EU to improve relations with Iran.
- A young woman in Iran, accused of having committed a crime when she was a minor, has now been executed.
