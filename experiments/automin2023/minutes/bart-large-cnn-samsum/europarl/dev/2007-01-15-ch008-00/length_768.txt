SUMMARY
- There is a new political group in the European Parliament called 'Identity, Traditions and Sovereignty'.
- It is composed of twenty Members from more than a fifth of the Member States.
- The rules of the Parliament require that the members of a political group share a political affinity.
- Mrs Mussolini's interview about the new group was contrary to Rule 29 of the Rules of Procedure.
- The House's procedural committee will examine the legitimacy of the group's formation.
- Parliament does not evaluate the political affinity of members of a group.
- The group has made a declaration of principles or political statement.
- This declaration will be published in the Official Journal and signed by all the members of the group.
- Mr Gollnisch cannot accede to Mr Schulz' request.
