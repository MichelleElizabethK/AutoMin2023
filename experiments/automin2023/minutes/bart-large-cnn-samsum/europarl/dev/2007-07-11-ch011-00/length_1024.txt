SUMMARY
- Parliament presents its second ever report on the economic situation in the Eurozone in response to the Annual Report of the European Commission.
- The economic growth rate rose to 2.7%, unemployment fell to 7.6%, the lowest in 15 years.
- Parliament stresses the importance of good governance and intensified policy coordination.
- Parliament is pleased at the decision taken by the Euro group in April this year to adopt benchmarks for the fiscal policies of Member States.
- The report on the eurozone was adopted unanimously by the Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs.
- In 2006 the economic recovery in the eurozone became a self-sustaining process with domestic demand acting as the main driver.
- Small economies have performed better than large economies since the creation of the eurozone in terms of growth.
- The report expresses concern about the appreciation of the euro against most major European currencies.
- In Ireland house prices fell by 2.1% between January and May 2007 and further decline is expected.
- Sub-prime lending is projected to grow to EUR 4 billion in Ireland this year.
- The number of banknotes in circulation stood at 11.3 billion with a value of EUR 628.2 billion last year.
- 500 euro banknotes alone have increased by 13.2%.
- The European Union has had the lowest unemployment level for a long time.
- Europe's public finances have improved since 2005-2006.
- The corrective arm of the reformed Stability and Growth Pact has proven itself.
- Eurogroup members are committed to the effectiveness of the policies that must be implemented in order to achieve the medium-term objective.
- PERSON261: France has been given reassurances regarding a reduction in the deficit in 2008, as compared with 2007, regarding the principle of allocating profits from revenue made in relation to budget estimates, and regarding the reduction of the deficit and the debt.
- The strength of the euro is another subject that gives rise to debate, but the ministers of Eurogroup do not claim that a strong euro is liable to jeopardise economic growth in the eurozone.
- Member States that are uneasy about the euro's strength should introduce structural reforms within their countries.
- In 2006 the euro area economy expanded at the highest growth rate since 2000.
- Real GDP rose by 2.9% compared with 1.5% in 2005.
- In 2006 average annual HICP inflation was 2.2%.
- In 2007 inflation has been slightly below 2%.
- The outlook for price stability over the medium term remains subject to upside risks.
- The Governing Council will continue to monitor all developments.
- There are economic divergences in the euro area.
- Inflation differences among euro area countries have fallen.
- The report on highly-leveraged institutions was issued in May 2007 by the Financial Stability Forum at the request of the G8.
- In April 2007, the European Parliament launched an official public consultation on the principles and proposals underlying the T2S Project.
- For the first time, the Council and the Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs are holding a debate on the situation of the eurozone.
- Many European citizens are not satisfied with the single currency, but the eurozone's economy is recovering.
- Eurozone's two leading economies, France and Germany, are no longer in a situation of excessive deficit.
- Excessive deficit procedures are currently still in place for two countries of the zone, Italy and Portugal.
- The application of the Stability and Growth Pact is being carried out satisfactorily.
- It is the preventive part of the Pact that we are concerned with now.
- The French President has promised to promote a serious programme of reforms between now and September.
- He has also promised to allocate all of the budget surplus to fiscal consolidation and debt reduction.
- The new Treaty will give the Eurogroup more institutional capacity.
- PERSON266 thanks rapporteurs Mitchell and Rosati for their work.
- Montesquieu's advice to apply the spirit of the law should be respected even if the governments have changed.
- He urges Juncker and Trichet to stick to their guns on the euro.
- Mr Juncker and his evening visitor should not be taken advantage of by Mr Trichet and Mr Almunia.
- Mr Mitchell's report on the Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs makes some important progress and advances.
- The report also calls for more caution regarding a possible rise in interest rates and for a minimum wage.
