DATE : 2023-05-14

SUMMARY
- The Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection recommends adopting the Council common position for adopting a directive of the European Parliament and of the Council on the Directive 76/211/EEC (13484/1/2006 - C6-0039/2007).
- The European Parliament retained mandatory sizes for a number of products, in permanent derogation in relation to the general liberalisation of sizes that was proposed by the Commission.
- The Commission and Finnish Presidency adopted a common position that took up some of the derogations requested by Parliament.
- Vice-President of the Commission (DE) thanks Mr Toubon for his constructive work on the legislative proposal on packaging sizes.
- He hopes that the Council will follow Parliament's example and carry out an impact assessment when making amendments to legislative proposals.
- The proposal will have a positive impact on consumers and businesses.
- PERSON174 congratulates Commissioner Jacques Toubon on becoming a Vice-President.
- The Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection will support the amendments to the European Commission's proposal to deregulate the market.
- People with disabilities, especially the partially sighted, should be aware of the consequences of deregulating the market and should be able to take advantage of it.
- Pre-packed loaves of bread in the United Kingdom will not be affected.
- The ALDE Group supports the rapporteur's amendments to the Packaging Directive.
- The rapporteur, Mr Toubon, has negotiated a compromise with Members of the European Parliament and with the Council.
- Today is 9 May, which is a day of peace for Europe.
- The Swedish members of the Christian Democrats and European Democrats are opposed to the Directive.
