DATE : 2023-05-14
ATTENDEES : PERSON036, PERSON037, PERSON038, PERSON039, PERSON040, PERSON041, PERSON042, PERSON043


SUMMARY
- The Committee on Budgetary Control is debating the nomination of David Bostock, Michel Cretin, Maarten B. Engwirda, Henri Grethen, Harald Noack, Ioannis Sarmas and Hubert Weber as members of the Court of Auditors.
- The Court is composed of 27 members from the Member States of the Union.
- The Committee on Budgetary Control considered the experience of Mr Sarmas, Mr Engwirda and Mr Weber to be a plus.
- Mr Weber is currently President of the Court of Auditors.
- He should be allowed to speak in order to clarify a matter that arose after the confirmation hearing.
- PERSON037 wants to know if the statements by Mr Weber and Mr Kallas are his and if not, he wants him to publish a denial and send a copy to the House.
- He is frustrated that the Council has ignored the criteria laid down by Parliament in its resolution of 1992.
- Good relations between Parliament and the Court of Auditors are essential if the control system is to function properly.
- The Parliament should study the Court's self-assessment exercise at the end of 2008 and take up a position on the various options.
- The Court should use all means at its disposal to rationalise and simplify its work.
- Today the European Parliament is going to vote on the nominations for seven new members of the Court of Auditors.
- The Parliament has no powers over the Member States and the selection they make at the national level.
- The majority of the nominations carried during the Committee voting is a testimony to the high appreciation the Parliament gave to each of these nominees.
- PERSON040 welcomes the proposals for the appointment of seven members of the Court of Auditors.
- He is concerned about the number and the profile of the members and the quality of the candidates.
- Mr Weber will respond to the appeal made by the rapporteur for this debate.
- PERSON042 clarifies that in a BBC interview, he did not mention Mrs Andreasen's name.
- He did not agree with Mr Kallas's statement that 40% of expenditure had been given the green light.
- The joint debate is now closed.
- The vote will take place this morning at 11 am.
