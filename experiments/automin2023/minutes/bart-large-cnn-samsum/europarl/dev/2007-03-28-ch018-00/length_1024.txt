SUMMARY
- The main idea behind the budget for 2008 is to keep expenditure more or less at 2007 levels.
- The level of inflation needs to be raised to 2007 levels for buildings.
- The local media should be able to visit Parliament more often and MEPs should invite more representatives of the local media.
- Parliament's reputation needs to improve by 2008.
- The European Parliament's current consumption of paper is nearly 850 tonnes a year, or 3.4 million sheets a week.
- The proposal envisages raising the air temperature of the European Parliament by a few degrees in summer.
- The Parliament recently adopted a statute on the assistants employed by MEPs.
- The 2008 budget emphasises the importance of the instruments used to better inform European citizens.
- Parliament's expenditure has grown rapidly due to the enlargement of the Union and the need for interpretation and translation services as well as space.
- 60% of the costs are due to multilingualism and a policy of having several places of business.
- The Parliament should keep the same budgeting level as in 2007.
- The benchmark of 20% of heading five should be retained as the budget's upper limit.
