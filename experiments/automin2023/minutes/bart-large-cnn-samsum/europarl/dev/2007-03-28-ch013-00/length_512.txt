DATE : 2023-05-14

SUMMARY
- The Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs wanted to have a debate on supervision among the European institutions.
- They are pleased about the relaunch of discussions and work on the management of the crisis on the financial markets.
- They support the establishment of a Committee of Wise Men to examine the implications of the consolidation of markets and of financial institutions.
- There have been profound changes in the financial system in Europe and around the world.
- The consolidation of financial markets has made it possible for key actors to establish themselves and to act on transnational bases.
- The European Union's financial system is characterised by diversity and by the wealth of operators operating across frontiers.
- PERSON079: Improvement of the supervisory systems is in the interests of all the players.
- Europe's lawmakers face a major challenge: to endow Europe with solid and efficient prudential structures capable of ensuring the proper supervision of all financial actors and contribute to the European model on the world stage.
- Mrs Berès raised a number of important issues in her speech.
- The European financial system plays a pivotal role in strengthening the financial-stability framework in the EU.
- The efficiency of financial supervision should be enhanced without encumbering the financial sector with an excessive supervisory burden or restricting competition.
- The Council supports the Commission's intention to use its powers to also ensure compliance with the rules on competition and state aid.
- The action plan approved by the Council in May of last year comprises a combination of several tools aimed at fostering the creation of a European supervisory culture.
- The Financial Services Committee has been asked to monitor the progress achieved by the three level 3 committees in implementing the various tools.
- The FSC recently set up a new sub-group, which is due to present a report on long-term supervisory issues by autumn 2007.
- The overall issue of supervisory convergence must be seen in the context of the consolidation of markets and financial institutions.
- Parliament and the Council agreed on the text of the Directive on the supervisory assessment of acquisitions in the financial sector as early as March.
- The European financial sector has changed dramatically over the last few years.
- The Commission has created European committees of supervisors in the areas of securities, banking and insurance.
- It has already led to greater supervisory cooperation and more convergence in supervisory practices.
- In investment services, there is the principle of more central control with some limited exceptions for branches.
- There will be a Commission conference on 26 June.
- The Commission's efforts have to be seen in conjunction with the work undertaken in the Ecofin Council.
- The Council will reflect in 2007 on how better to address cross-border crisis situations and clarify burden-sharing arrangements.
- The Financial Services Committee has initiated work on issues related to improvement of supervisory efficiency.
- The Interinstitutional Monitoring Committee on the Lamfalussy process will complete its final report in 2007.
- The Commission will produce its own assessment of the way in which the Lamfoulussy process works towards the end of the year.
- Mrs Berès' question on the Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs is being discussed and answered here today.
- The crisis simulation with a view to reviewing financial stability in the EU has revealed shortcomings.
- Consumer protection, an effective financial industry and stable financial markets constitute the supreme objective of financial supervision.
- Good supervision must be guided by the existing risks and take a principle-based approach instead of devoting itself to detailed individual analysis.
- Parliament has adopted a report on the supervision of European financial markets.
- The report questions whether the present system ensures the effective supervision of large groups that operate in various countries and sectors.
- Parliament agreed on the need for a committee of experts to study these implications and report back with their recommendations.
- PERSON084 reminds the European Commission and Council to prepare for winter during the summer.
- Financial groups operate in all European Union countries and there is an increased systematic risk in the market.
- The reference point for reforming supervisory processes has to be what we would do in a crisis situation.
- The Financial Services Action Plan is now more or less complete.
- The focus is now on implementation and supervisory convergence.
- For top pan-European players, we should have a well-equipped European supervisory authority inside the system.
- It is also important to develop cooperation at global level.
- PERSON086 thanks all the members of the Commission for their valuable contributions.
- The development of financial supervision is of critical importance.
- A stable financial environment is a prerequisite for the economic growth that the EU needs and the protection of consumers.
- Supervision needs to be efficient and effective to prevent financial crises.
