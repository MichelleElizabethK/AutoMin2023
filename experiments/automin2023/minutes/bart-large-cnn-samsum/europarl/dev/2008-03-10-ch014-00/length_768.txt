SUMMARY
- There will be a discussion on the Lamfalussy process on Tuesday afternoon.
- Commissioner Almunia will present the Commission's views in that debate.
- The PPE-DE Group wants the second item, on Afghanistan, to be replaced by a new item entitled 'Arrests of demonstrators following the presidential elections in Russia'.
- Francis Wurtz, the President of Afghanistan, has been sentenced to death for downloading an article on the rights of women in Islam.
- The House will vote on whether to put the case of the Afghan on the agenda.
- The ALDE and GUE Groups have tabled a joint request for the inclusion of a new item dealing with the Iranian Mehdi Kazemi.
- The PPE-DE Group has also requested that the cases of Archbishop Paulos Faraj Rahho and Mr Schulz should be included.
- There are three items on the agenda: Afghanistan, the Iranian homosexual in mortal danger, the Archbishop and the Russian issue.
- Under the Rules of Procedure, the President has to choose between the three.
- The GUE/NGL Group and the PPE-DE Group want to have a debate on the Afghan case first.
- The President will make an exception for this proposal.
