SUMMARY
- The oral question to the Commission on milk quotas is on behalf of the PPE-DE Group - B6-0444/2006.
- It asks if the current system of milk quotas will be maintained in its current form until 2015.
- The Azores, which accounts for 30% of Portuguese milk production, is sensitive to the changes.
- The quota system provides some stability for the European market.
- The value of the quota system varies between Member States.
- Quota levels per Member State are fixed until 31 March 2015.
- After that date, the system of milk quotas will end.
- The most promising long-term scenario for European dairy sector is one without quotas.
- The decision to retain the quota until 31 March 2015 is a cause of vigorous debate, particularly in certain regions dependent on milk and on the quotas.
- Mr President, Commissioner and Mr Freitas should have a debate in good time on what they propose to do about the situation.
- PERSON137 thanks the Christian Democrats, European Democrats and Mr Freitas for their reflections on the Commission statements on milk quotas.
- He points out the importance of the milk sector in Europe at the level of production, employment, investments, foreign trade and food security.
- The current preconditions create oligopolies in the sector.
- The Commissioner will submit an honest proposal as regards the limits.
- PERSON138 thanks Mr Freitas for taking the initiative and raising this important topic this evening.
- He is in agreement with the Commissioner's statement that it is important for farmers to know where they stand.
- The Commissioner's position on the milk quota system is most worrying, in that she suggests it might be dismantled by 2015.
- PERSON141 asks the Commissioner to clarify the situation of milk quotas in Ireland.
- The Minister for Agriculture discriminates against older quota owners in favour of young farmers.
- European dairy policy is becoming snarled up.
- Europe has lost 60% of its exports within the global market and lost 4% of world production.
- Europe needs to come up with a timetable after 2015.
- PERSON143 wants to know if the mid-term reform of the CAP is working and if the Commission intends to extend the current rules to the 2014-2015 financial year.
- He thinks that the sector needs stability and not shocks, time to adapt itself and to plan.
- The European Commission is reviewing the common agricultural policy and milk quotas.
- Poland has a quota of 9 million tonnes of milk compared to the 13 to 13.5 million tonnes it should be allowed.
- Galicia, a milk-producing region, is worried about changes to the system established in 2003 for the milk sector.
- PERSON149 thanks Commissioner Commissioner for his frequent attendance in Strasbourg.
- He wants Commissioner to adopt a strategy of clarification for dairy farmers and make it clear to the sector that the Commission honours its agreements and the arrangements fixed until 2014.
- He hopes Commissioner will take the opportunity to engage with them in the Committee on Agriculture and be involved in ensuring a future for the dairy sector.
- PERSON151 and PERSON152 are worried about the future of the quota system for milk production in the EU.
- The Commissioner is going to do a health check on the common agricultural policy next year.
- The current system inhibits the development of the dairy sector and makes it less competitive on the world market.
- Mr President, in Ireland they speak of little else but dairy farmers.
- They want to know what is going to be done from 2008 to collapse the value of the milk quota.
- Mr President is looking at increasing quotas from the health check onwards, reducing super-levy costs to Member States, balancing quotas across the European Union and cross-border trading.
- PERSON154 thanks Mr Freitas for bringing the debate forward and the Commissioner for being here tonight.
- He wants to know if the phasing out of dairy quotas can begin before 2015 and if it will begin from 2015 onwards.
- He also wants the Commissioner's opinion on the issue.
- PERSON156 is a member of the Commission.
- The quota system was introduced in 1984 to maintain high prices for dairy products.
- It will expire in 2015.
- There will be a health check on the quota system and a report on the dairy market outlook report at the end of next year.
- There are reports that the Commission is preparing to reform the organisation of the milk market and abolish milk quotas.
- The current regulations for the milk sector are in force until 2015 and the quota system has been providing the market and producers with stability.
- In Hungary milk production is declining due to problems of efficiency.
- Hungary is using only around 70% of the Community quota allocated to it.
