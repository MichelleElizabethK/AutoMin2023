SUMMARY
- The Committee on Internal Market and Consumer Protection recommends that the European Parliament adopt the Council common position, modified by some amendments.
- The European Parliament retained mandatory sizes for a number of products, but for a limited period, a transitional period.
- The Vice-President of the Commission agrees with the rapporteur's proposal to simplify and harmonise the regulations on packaging sizes.
- He hopes the Council will follow Parliament's example and do an impact assessment when making amendments to legislative proposals.
- Parliament's Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection supports the Commission's aims of deregulating the market and removing unnecessary legislation.
- It was one of the first committees in Parliament to commission an impact assessment on its proposed amendments.
- Jacques Toubon led an outstanding review of the process.
- The European Parliament is debating a new law on packaging sizes for bread.
- It is important to make sure that the nominal quantities will not be abolished automatically after a certain time.
- The ALDE Group supports the idea of transitional periods for certain categories of product.
- The Swedish Members of the European People's Party (Christian Democrats) and European Democrats are opposed to the Packaging Directive.
