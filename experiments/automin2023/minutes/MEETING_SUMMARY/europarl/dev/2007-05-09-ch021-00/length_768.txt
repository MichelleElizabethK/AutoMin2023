SUMMARY
- The next item is the report on the public health consequences of the 1968 Thule crash (petition 720/2002).
- The Commission has concluded that the claims brought by the petitioners cannot be based on Community law and solely on the basis of Danish national legislation.
- The petitioners and workers who cleaned up after the tragedy want the European Commission to help them achieve a political solution.
- The US B-52 crash in Greenland was very fortunate.
- The relevant authorities were ill-prepared and did not deal with the problem properly.
- The Danish Government refused to provide access to the relevant environmental records needed to estimate the radiation doses received by affected workers.
- After listening to the Commissioner, PERSON226 thanks rapporteur Diana Wallis for her excellent report on the Thule nuclear accident.
- The Committee on Petitions hopes the report will be properly implemented and will have some significance.
- The plane crash on 21 January 1968 in North-Western Greenland triggered an extensive radioactive pollution.
- It took 18 years for the Danish authorities to take an interest in the health of the people affected and of the civilian workers involved in the clean-up operation.
